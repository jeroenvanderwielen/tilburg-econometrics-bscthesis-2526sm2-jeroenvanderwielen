For a business serving customers, long-term profitability does not only depend on acquiring customers, but on whether the revenue generated by each customer over time exceeds the costs of acquiring and serving them. The Customer Lifetime Value (CLV) formalizes this concept: it represents the present value of the expected future profits from a customer over the duration of their relationship with the firm. The CLV relies on the probability that a customer remains active and the time value of money. Therefore, it generates more insights for a business than simple profit margins, and provides a measure of how much customers are worth to a business at a certain point in time. \\

CLV provides businesses with direct insight into which customer segments are most valuable and whether acquisition costs are justified. However, estimating CLV is not straightforward in most cases. When only a limited history of longitudinal data is available, challenges such as right-censored observations make standard approaches unreliable. Therefore, it is important to choose the right method to estimate CLV for a business. \\

In this thesis, an estimation will be made of the CLV of MarleenKookt, an established Dutch meal delivery company based in Amsterdam that has served over 14,000 households with fresh food since 2011. In ... 2024 (ask Joris when they exactly started commercializing this concept, there are a lot of test entries in the data that make it unclear when the subscription service exactly started), MarleenKookt launched a subscription service that allows customers to receive recurring weekly meal deliveries without placing individual orders each time. The estimation of the CLV focuses only on the subscription data from MarleenKookt, because the subscription model is new and it represents a contractual customer relationship, in contrast to the non-contractual individual order customer relationship. \\

The time horizon of the relevant subscription data only covers 20 months, which makes the estimation of CLV difficult. However, an accurate estimation of the CLV is important to a company like MarleenKookt to evaluate and grow its new subscription model. Therefore, the central question of this thesis is stated as follows: What is the expected CLV of MarleenKookt subscription customers, and how sensitive are these estimates to different modeling approaches given the limited time horizon of the available data?
